1. The Problem — Why This Exists
This startup idea emerged from a keynote by Matthew Prince, CEO of Cloudflare, at South by Southwest 2026. Prince handles 20% of all web traffic and provided a clear, data-backed picture of a massive and growing problem.

1.1 What Matthew Prince Said (The Evidence)
"By 2027, the amount of bot traffic online will exceed the amount of human traffic." — Matthew Prince, Cloudflare CEO, SXSW 2026

•Bot traffic is no longer minor. The internet has historically been ~20% bot traffic. That ratio is now inverting.
•AI agents visit 1,000x more pages than humans. If a human shops for a camera on 5 websites, an AI agent will visit 5,000.
•The old internet business model is broken. Bots don't click ads. Bots don't subscribe. The model of "create content, drive traffic, sell ads/subscriptions" completely fails in an AI world.
•Even the world's best retailers don't know the answer. Walmart lets all bots in freely. Amazon is suing companies to keep bots out. Target only allows their own bots. Three radically different strategies — no consensus.
•AI companies want unique local content and will pay for it. The Park Record (a small local newspaper in Utah owned by Prince and his wife) may earn more from AI content licensing in 2026 than from digital advertising.

1.2 The Core Technical Problem
When an AI agent visits a publisher's website, it hits the origin server directly — bypassing CDN caches because agents need "fresh" content. A training scraper like GPTBot will crawl an entire site in a single session, generating:
•Massive origin server CPU usage
•Enormous bandwidth consumption — 40% of a publisher's bandwidth can be eaten by training crawlers alone
•Zero revenue — bots don't click ads, buy products, or subscribe
•13% of bots now bypass robots.txt entirely, mimicking human browser sessions

The problem is simple: publishers are subsidising the training of global AI models with their own infrastructure budgets. Every bot visit costs real money and generates nothing in return.

2. XOXO — The Solution
2.1 What XOXO Does
XOXO is an intelligent compute savings layer for publishers. It sits between AI bots and a publisher's origin server, intercepting bot traffic at the edge, serving cached responses, and ensuring the publisher's servers only handle traffic that's worth handling.

One line: XOXO is the product that cuts your server bill by 30–60% as AI bot traffic grows — automatically, silently, without touching your codebase.

2.2 Business Model — The Pivot That Matters
The initial concept considered charging AI companies per-token for content access (like TollBit). After analysis, XOXO pivoted to a cleaner, more defensible model:

Old model (v1)
Charge AI companies for access — revenue from AI bots paying per token
Problem with v1
AI companies can ignore the paywall. Compliance is unenforceable. TollBit already does this. Cloudflare is building it natively.
New model (v2)
Charge publishers a SaaS fee for compute savings. Revenue = money saved on servers, CDN, and origin costs.
Why v2 wins
Entirely within XOXO's control. No AI company cooperation needed. Measurable, predictable, self-reinforcing ROI.

2.3 Revenue Model
•Pure SaaS subscription from publishers
•Pricing based on monthly traffic volume
•Three tiers: Starter ($99/mo up to 5M visits), Publisher ($499/mo up to 50M visits), Enterprise (custom)
•ROI framing: "Pay a fraction of what you save" — a publisher saving $8,000/month pays $499 for the product, a 16× ROI
•Churn protection: cancelling XOXO causes server bills to spike immediately — the savings are tangible and visible

3. How XOXO Works — The Technical Architecture
3.1 The Two-Phase Cache Model
XOXO does NOT require installation on the publisher's server. It works at the edge — between the internet and the publisher's origin. 

Phase 1: Cold Cache (First bot visit to a URL)
When a bot first visits any URL on a publisher's site:
1.XOXO Worker intercepts the request
2.Classifies it as a bot (training scraper, retrieval agent, search crawler, or disguised bot)
3.Passes the request through to origin (necessary — no cached copy exists yet)
4.Stores the response in XOXO's edge cache, keyed to that URL
5.Serves the response to the bot
Phase 2: Warm Cache (Every subsequent bot request)
For every subsequent bot visit to the same URL:
6.XOXO Worker intercepts the request
7.Classifies it as a bot
8.Finds cached response in XOXO's edge cache
9.Serves cached response — origin server never touched. No CPU. No bandwidth. No cost.
This is where the savings come from. Training scrapers visit the same pages thousands of times. Every visit after the first costs nothing because it is served from XOXO's edge cache, not the publisher's server.

3.2 What XOXO Caches and Serves
•Full page HTML — for training scrapers that need raw content
•Structured lightweight JSON — key content, title, metadata only. 10x smaller than full HTML. What retrieval agents actually need.
•llms.txt — auto-generated machine-readable site guide. Agents can get all the structural information they need from this single file, without crawling 5,000 pages.

3.3 Cache Freshness
Cache TTL (time-to-live) is configurable — typically 1–24 hours for article content. Publishers can also fire a webhook to purge specific URLs instantly when content is updated. This is the same pattern publishers already use for their CDN caches — XOXO is not introducing a new concept.

3.4 Where the Cache Lives
XOXO's edge cache is NOT on the publisher's server. It runs on XOXO's own globally distributed edge network, built on top of Cloudflare's infrastructure. When a bot hits a publisher's site from San Francisco, the XOXO Worker intercepts it at the nearest Cloudflare PoP (San Francisco), checks XOXO's edge cache, and returns the response — the publisher's Mumbai origin server never wakes up.

4. How Publishers Connect — Client Integration
4.1 Method 1: Cloudflare Worker (Recommended — 5 Minutes)
For publishers already behind Cloudflare (most serious publishers are):
•Go to Cloudflare Dashboard → Workers & Pages → Create
•Paste the XOXO Worker script (50 lines of JavaScript, provided on signup)
•Add a route: yourdomain.com/*
•Done — no code changes to the actual website

The Worker intercepts every request, calls XOXO's edge classify API, and based on the response either:
–Serves a cached response (bot, cached URL)
–Returns a 403 block (blocked bot)
–Passes through to origin (human or allowed bot)

4.2 Method 2: Reverse Proxy / Snippet (Any CDN — 15 Minutes)
For publishers on Fastly, Vercel, AWS CloudFront, or nginx — a config block that fires a subrequest to XOXO before passing traffic to origin. No website code changes required.

4.3 Key Integration Insight
XOXO sits entirely outside the publisher's stack. Their website code is untouched. Zero deployment risk. Reversible in 30 seconds. This is a critical selling point — there is no engineering risk in trying XOXO.

8.3 How Copyable Is the Tech?
The tech itself is not hard to copy. Any competent team can build the core product in 3–4 months. The real moats are:
•Data flywheel: More publishers → more bot traffic signals → better classification accuracy → happier publishers → more publishers. A competitor needs to break into this loop from zero.
•Savings lock-in: Every month a publisher stays, their server bill goes down. Switching away means bills spike immediately. The longer they stay, the more painful leaving becomes.
•Speed of market: Bot traffic doubles every 12–18 months. Every month delayed is a month a competitor can sign 50 publishers. Move fast while the window is open.
•Integration depth: After 6 months, publisher ops runbooks, cost projections, and Cloudflare configs all assume XOXO exists. Ripping it out has organisational friction.
The tech is the entry ticket. The business — publisher relationships, fingerprint dataset, and integration depth — is the moat. This is exactly why moving fast in year one matters more than building perfect tech.

9. Product — User Journey & Dashboard Design
9.1 The Complete User Journey
The full journey from discovery to active savings has been designed and prototyped:

10.Sign up — Email/password or Google/GitHub SSO
11.Choose plan — Starter (free), Publisher ($499/mo), Enterprise (custom)
12.Add domain — Single input, auto-detects Cloudflare, surfaces right connection method
13.Install Worker — Paste 50 lines of JS into Cloudflare Dashboard, add route
14.Verify — 6-point checklist confirms everything is working
15.First savings moment — Big green number, dark screen, emotional payoff. This is the screen that sells.
16.Dashboard — Live, full-featured dashboard with savings compounding daily

The first savings moment is the most important screen in the product. When a publisher sees "$0.12 saved in the last 60 seconds — 847 bots intercepted, 0 origin hits" — that is the moment they become a long-term customer. Design this screen with more care than any other.

9.2 Dashboard Pages
•Overview — Headline metrics (savings, origin calls blocked, bot share, bandwidth saved), daily savings chart, traffic donut, top bots table
•Traffic — Human vs bot trend, requests by category, peak traffic hours
•Savings — Hero savings number, breakdown by compute/bandwidth/CDN, cumulative savings chart, projected annual savings
•Bot Intelligence — All identified bots, classification accuracy, disguised bot alerts, compliance status
•Policy — Global toggles, default rules by category (training/retrieval/search/unknown), per-bot overrides. Changes take effect in 30 seconds. No deploys.
•Settings — Domain config, cache TTL, plan & billing with usage meter, integrations (Cloudflare, Google Analytics, Slack, Webhook), API key

9.3 The Savings Dashboard — The Core Retention Mechanic
The savings dashboard is not just a nice-to-have. It is XOXO's primary retention mechanism. Every month the publisher sees:
•Exactly how much XOXO saved them this month (broken down by compute, bandwidth, CDN)
•What they paid for XOXO this month
•Their ROI (typically 10–30x in month one, growing as cache warms)
•Projected annual savings at current growth rate

Cancelling XOXO means this savings number immediately returns to zero and their server bill spikes. The dashboard makes that consequence viscerally clear every month.
